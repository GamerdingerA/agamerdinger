My research concentrates on three empirical areas. These are algorithmic markets, professional work and ethics, and public-sector AI governance. I draw on economic sociology, science and technology studies, and the sociology of professions to analyze how algorithmic systems are made workable and legitimate in markets, organizations, and public institutions.

## Workstreams

<section class="workstream-strip">
	<article class="workstream-card">
		<div class="workstream-eyebrow">Workstream 1</div>
		<h3>Algorithmic Markets</h3>
		<p class="workstream-question">How do algorithmic systems take root in some market contexts and not in others, and what does this mean for people's life chances?</p>
		<ul>
			<li>Uneven algorithmization and the dynamics that shape why some algorithms take root in organizations and reshape fields, while others fail to gain traction.</li>
			<li>A focus on markets that shape people's life chances, including insurance, credit, and the financial sector.</li>
			<li>Particular attention to <em>moral markets</em>, where legitimation is a central principle of market exchange relations.</li>
		</ul>
	</article>
	<article class="workstream-card">
		<div class="workstream-eyebrow">Workstream 2</div>
		<h3>Professional Work, Ethics, and Authority</h3>
		<p class="workstream-question">How do professional cultures and ethical frameworks shape algorithmic practice, and how is AI reshaping professional expertise and boundaries?</p>
		<ul>
			<li>How professional cultures and ethical frameworks shape practice within algorithmic markets.</li>
			<li>How AI and generative AI alter professional expertise, shift professional boundaries, and reshape collaboration within organizations and markets.</li>
			<li>Empirical focus on data scientists as a novel profession, operating in more fluid and less institutionalized ways than established professions.</li>
		</ul>
	</article>
	<article class="workstream-card">
		<div class="workstream-eyebrow">Workstream 3</div>
		<h3>Public-Sector AI Governance</h3>
		<p class="workstream-question">How do states orchestrate AI development, and how do public-private coalitions enable AI to scale beyond pilot projects?</p>
		<ul>
			<li>The infrastructures, coalitions, and agreements between private and public actors that shape the co-constitution of technology and society.</li>
			<li>How states orchestrate the development and scaling of AI systems beyond pilot projects.</li>
			<li>How Nordic countries and other AI middle powers establish visions of a common good and shape what an AI society can become.</li>
		</ul>
	</article>
</section>

## Methods

My projects use mixed methods including interviews, ethnographic observation, document analysis, and quantitative techniques such as statistical modelling, network analysis, and multiple correspondence analysis.
